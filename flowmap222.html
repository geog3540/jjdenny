Interpretation of Flow Patterns

Design Choice: I used graduated circles that vary in size and color from light orange to a darker red, that are proportional to the total number of bikers visiting each station.

Justification:

  • I used graduated circles that represent quantitative point data (Koylu, C. & Guo, D. (2017)). Larger circles naturally draw attention to high-traffic stations, which aligns with visual hierarchy principles.
  • The color gradient (orange to red) provides a sense of intensity, which red is commonly associated with "hotspots" or higher volumes (Koylu, C. & Guo, D. (2017)).
  • This approach moves away from FlowMapper’s default choropleth node zones by using directly proportional symbology, which allows immediate visual comparison of station popularity without needing to reference a background polygon.

Flow Attractors (Receivers):

  • The Downtown and Near North Side areas are the main attractors, which receive the highest volume of incoming flows. This is indicated by the thicker and darker blue flow lines that converge on large, dark red nodes in the city core.
  • Stations near Millennium Park, Navy Pier, and the Loop have especially high inflow, because of their proximity to tourist destinations, employment centers, and public transit.

Flow Senders (Origins):

  • Because downtown sends high volumes of flows, some smaller stations on the city’s west and northwest sides appear to act more as origins than destinations.
  • Neighborhoods like West Town, Logan Square, and areas bordering West Garfield Park show medium to large-sized stations with several outgoing lines, which suggests that it’s more a commuter area from the suburbs to downtown.

General Flow Direction Patterns:

  • The dominant flow direction converges toward the central and lakeside areas from nearly all other directions, especially from the west and northwest.
  • There is a definite north-south corridor along the lakeshore that connects stations from Lincoln Park down to the South Loop, which shows recreational and commuter use along this accessible path.
  • There are fewer east-west connections in the southern part of the map, probably due to lower station density and residential zoning further south.
